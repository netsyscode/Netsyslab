Biography

The laboratory has conducted more than a decade of fundamental theoretical research and core technological breakthroughs in the field of secure and controllable IPv6 next-generation internet architecture. Numerous papers have been published in top-tier academic conferences (INFOCOM, ICNP, IWQoS) and journals (TON, TPDS, TIFS, CN, JNCA) in the network domain. The research outcomes have been deeply applied in network infrastructure construction and operation. In recent years, significant research achievements have been made in areas such as routing and transmission mechanism optimization, network/system anomaly detection, and network situational awareness, including:

1:) Routing and Transmission Mechanism Optimization: For different scenarios such as data centers, intra-domain, and inter-domain, research has been conducted on optimization models, methods, and efficient algorithms for routing and transmission mechanisms. This mainly includes intra-domain/inter-domain multipath algorithms, fast rerouting methods, traffic engineering methods, data center congestion control algorithms, and single-stream/multi-stream scheduling algorithms.

2:) System Anomaly Detection: Focusing on network traffic and various systems, research has been conducted on anomaly detection and localization using machine learning, deep learning, optimization theory, and formal testing. A series of mechanisms for anomaly detection, localization, and root cause analysis have been proposed.

3:) Network Situational Awareness: Based on large datasets from systems or security, research has been conducted to sense, analyze, and predict environmental factors, user behavior, and various events in temporal or spatial dimensions. This has improved network and system resource utilization, optimized user experience, and ensured system security.
